Parallel & Perpendicular Lines

Lesson 4.9 — how the slopes of two lines reveal how they meet

Integration Academy

Learning goals

What you'll be able to do

  • ✅ Decide whether two lines are parallel from their slopes.
  • ✅ Decide whether two lines are perpendicular from their slopes.
  • ✅ Write the equation of a line that is parallel or perpendicular to a given line through a chosen point.
Quick recap

Everything hinges on the slope

A line in slope-intercept form:

y = m·x + b

  • m = slope = riserun
  • b = y-intercept (where it crosses the y-axis)

💡 To compare two lines, compare their slopes. That single number tells you if they are parallel, perpendicular, or neither.

Concept 1

Parallel lines

Parallel lines never meet. They have

m1 = m2

— the same slope, but different y-intercepts.

Same steepness ⇒ they run side-by-side forever.

Concept 1 · example

Are these parallel?

y = 2x + 1

y = 2x − 5

Both have slope 2; the y-intercepts (1 and −5) differ.

✔ Yes — parallel (and distinct).

Concept 2

Perpendicular lines

Perpendicular lines meet at 90°. Their slopes are negative reciprocals:

m1 · m2 = −1  ⇒  m2 = −1m1

Flip the fraction, change the sign.

slope 3  ⟂  slope −13

because 3 × (−13) = −1

See it together

Equal slopes ⇒ parallel · negative-reciprocal slopes ⇒ perpendicular

y = 2x + 1

y = 2x − 3  (parallel)

y = −½x + 1  (perpendicular)

The two blue lines share slope 2. The red line's slope −½ is the negative reciprocal of 2, so it crosses them at right angles.

Special case

Horizontal ⟂ vertical

  • A horizontal line y = c has slope 0.
  • A vertical line x = c has undefined slope.

We can't use m1·m2 = −1 here (one slope is undefined) — but they still meet at a perfect right angle. ⟂

Worked example 1 · parallel through a point

Line through (0, 1) parallel to y = 2x − 3

Step 1. Parallel ⇒ same slope = 2.

Step 2. It passes through (0, 1), so the y-intercept is 1.

y = 2x + 1

Worked example 2 · perpendicular through a point

Line through (0, −2) perpendicular to y = ½x + 4

Step 1. Given slope 12 ⇒ perpendicular slope = negative reciprocal = −2.

Step 2. Through (0, −2) ⇒ y-intercept −2.

y = −2x − 2

⭐ Advanced example

Is the line through (1, 2) and (3, 8) perpendicular to y = −⅓x?

Step 1. Slope through the points: 8 − 23 − 1 = 62 = 3.

Step 2. Multiply the slopes: 3 × (−13) = −1.

✔ Product is −1 ⇒ yes, perpendicular.

Your turn

Quick check

Q1. Slope of any line parallel to y = −5x + 1?

−5

Q2. Slope of any line perpendicular to y = 23x?

−32

Q3. Line through (0, 3) parallel to y = −x + 5?

y = −x + 3
Key takeaways

Remember this

Parallel ⇒ slopes are equal  (m1 = m2)

Perpendicular ⇒ slopes are negative reciprocals  (m1 · m2 = −1)

Horizontal & vertical lines are perpendicular (special case).
