\begin{ideabox}
An annuity is equal regular payments: $FV=R\dfrac{(1+i)^{n}-1}{i}$ and $PV=R\dfrac{1-(1+i)^{-n}}{i}$.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item $R=$ the regular payment.
  \item Future value: total at the end. Present value: worth today.
  \item An annuity is a geometric series.
\end{itemize}
\end{ideabox}

\begin{learnbox}
\lh{Regular payments}An \textbf{annuity} is a series of equal payments made at regular intervals, such as savings deposits or loan repayments.
\lh{Future and present value}The \textbf{future value} sums each payment's growth; the \textbf{present value} sums each payment discounted to today — both are geometric series.
\lh{Everyday uses}Annuity formulas model mortgages, car loans, and retirement savings, where equal payments meet compound interest.
\end{learnbox}

\sech{Worked Examples}

\begin{example}{ex0}{Example 1 --- Future value}
$\$500$/year for $4$ years at $5\%$.\soln $FV=500\cdot\dfrac{(1.05)^{4}-1}{0.05}=500\cdot\dfrac{0.215506}{0.05}=500(4.31013)\approx\$2155.06$.
\end{example}

\begin{example}{ex1}{Example 2 --- Future value}
$\$1000$/year for $3$ years at $6\%$.\soln $1000\cdot\dfrac{(1.06)^{3}-1}{0.06}=1000(3.1836)\approx\$3183.60$.
\end{example}

\begin{example}{ex2}{Example 3 --- Present value}
$\$200$/year for $5$ years at $5\%$.\soln $200\cdot\dfrac{1-(1.05)^{-5}}{0.05}=200(4.32948)\approx\$865.90$.
\end{example}

\begin{example}{ex3}{Example 4 --- Bigger payment}
$\$1200$/year for $4$ years at $4\%$ (future value).\soln $1200\cdot\dfrac{(1.04)^{4}-1}{0.04}=1200(4.246464)\approx\$5095.76$.
\end{example}

\begin{example}{ex4}{Example 5 --- FV vs PV}
Why is an annuity's present value smaller than its future value?\soln Future value includes all the interest still to be earned; present value discounts every payment back to today, so it is smaller.
\end{example}

\begin{example}{ex5}{Example 6 --- Future value}
$\$1000$/year for $4$ years at $5\%$.\soln $1000\cdot\dfrac{(1.05)^{4}-1}{0.05}\approx\$4310.13$.
\end{example}

\begin{example}{ex6}{Example 7 --- Future value}
$\$500$/year for $3$ years at $6\%$.\soln $500\cdot\dfrac{(1.06)^{3}-1}{0.06}\approx\$1591.80$.
\end{example}

\begin{example}{ex7}{Example 8 --- Present value}
$\$300$/year for $4$ years at $5\%$.\soln $300\cdot\dfrac{1-(1.05)^{-4}}{0.05}\approx\$1063.79$.
\end{example}

\begin{example}{ex8}{Example 9 --- Future value}
$\$2000$/year for $3$ years at $4\%$.\soln $2000\cdot\dfrac{(1.04)^{3}-1}{0.04}\approx\$6243.20$.
\end{example}
