\begin{ideabox}
On the unit circle the point at angle $\theta$ is $(\cos\theta,\sin\theta)$; this gives every special-angle value and the sign in each quadrant.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=2pt]
  \item $(\cos\theta,\sin\theta)$; $\tan\theta=\tfrac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$.
  \item $\sin\tfrac{\pi}{6}=\tfrac12,\ \cos\tfrac{\pi}{6}=\tfrac{\sqrt3}{2},\ \sin\tfrac{\pi}{4}=\cos\tfrac{\pi}{4}=\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2}$.
  \item CAST: which ratios are positive by quadrant.
\end{itemize}
\end{ideabox}

\begin{learnbox}
\lh{The unit circle}On the circle of radius $1$, the point at angle $\theta$ has coordinates $(\cos\theta,\ \sin\theta)$ — the definition that extends trig to every angle.
\lh{Exact values}The special angles $\tfrac{\pi}{6},\tfrac{\pi}{4},\tfrac{\pi}{3}$ and their multiples give exact coordinates worth knowing all around the circle.
\lh{Signs by quadrant}The signs of $\cos\theta$ and $\sin\theta$ follow the point's position, matching the CAST rule.
\end{learnbox}
